Love the Imperfection

  • Hai Son Nguyen
  • February 18, 2021
  • What I talk about Books
  • books, life advice, review, sách, self-help

|Illustration by Lorenzo Lanfranconi on ArtStation|

If I have to choose a book that helps me overcome challenging days in the past, it would be “Love for Imperfect Things: How to Accept Yourself in a World Striving for Perfection” by Haemin Sunim. It is a self-help book written by a Buddhist monk, but it is not about religion. While Haemin Sunim does include many meaningful insights from Buddhism, he keeps the texts of his book familiar and informal to reach a wide range of readers. The book is not a philosophical text but more like a piece of advice on how to live a happy and fulfilled life.

Firstly, Haemin Sunim wants to touch a large part of society considered “good” by the majority. A good child, a good student, a good friend. They are all relative terms. But Sunim has pointed out that we consider someone “good” because the person complied with others’ will and is not self-assertive. We see someone good mostly because they can suppress their desires in deference to others. But is this really a “good” person? Is he/she happy while being a good person? This is the first question that Sunim wants to clarify in his book. And the answer is surprising: Many good people do not live their lives so happily. They spend so much time thinking of others instead of expressing their own will. They may not even consider themselves “good” because they are merely trying desperately to conform to everyone, although we cannot and should not make everyone in the world like us. These self-judgments eventually make them depressed and anxious all the time. This type of person is not uncommon nowadays when parents and schools’ education directly encourage their children and students to listen and do what they are taught.

The change is not impossible. It does not have to be significant and enormous changes. It can start just from everyday situations like telling other people what you truly want to do instead of always following what you are said. You need to be kind to yourself first. Isn’t that what we are told to do to put on our oxygen mask before helping others when there is an airplane emergency? The same principle applies to our life. After all, it is not selfish at all to take care of ourselves first.

The rest of the book is various useful insights about different aspects of our life like family, relationships, work, and study. Haemin Sunim has succeeded in helping people heal their wounds and hurt feelings in this chaotic modern world. His words are like the arms of a beloved mother who always welcomes her children no matter what they do, how successful they are, or how failure they consider themselves to be. In 2018, there were 48,344 recorded suicides in the United States. The number is alarming to governments and health administrators around the world. We usually ignore these numbers and try to cover them with our default thinking like: These are just very rare in society. We are ordinary people, so it would be ok. Not until one day, the coworker who usually sits next to us disappeared when we realized how ignorant we had been to this social issue.

The book is a valuable source to read, especially in this uncertain time when pandemics and protests are happening all over the US. The waves of anxiety, hatred, and anger are spreading fast and indirectly kill people. Hopefully, after reading this book, you will have another view about your life and others’, so that you can be the one who has positive effects on the people around you. If every one of us may acknowledge that, we can all make the world a better place.

If you feel like this book fits you, then you can also check out another book by Haemin Sunim that I also keep with me ever since I bought it, “The Things You Can See Only When You Slow Down: How to Be Calm and Mindful in a Fast-Paced World.”

I hope what you read will console you somehow with all the good and bad things that are happening around you. And you will find yourself a quiet corner of the house to enjoy this beautiful book.

Love always,
Son
