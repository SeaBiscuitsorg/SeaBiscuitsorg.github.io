Running in the wild

  • Hai Son Nguyen
  • January 12, 2021
  • What I talk about Running
  • habit, running, trivial things

|Illustration by Ingram Schell on ArtStation|

These quarantine days help me to pick up a new habit: Running. I have been inspired after reading the book, “What I talk about when I talk about running” by the Japanese author Haruki Murakami. Murakami tells about his journey to participate in marathons by running every day, even how busy he is. And how running makes him into a better person: better health, better work productiveness, and better life enjoyment. The texts have been my underlying motivation to try running and see what is in it for me.

It is actually so simple to start running. It requires much less equipment and gears like some other activities and surely doesn’t need you to collect more than ten other people like for a soccer match. That is running’s convenience. You just start running. Or like me looking for a great trainer because I want to make this habit serious. But however prepared you are, the beginning is always difficult. I found a great trail that has few intersections or constructions and great views of my campus at UTD. The first day is great, and I just ran with all efforts I had. And it felt like on top of the world when you got home and feel really satisfied. The second day, well, okay. On the third day, I become so tired that I walk for the most part. And the fourth day, I ended up sleeping in until 9.

While I was struggling to develop a good schedule, my housemate suddenly invited me to train with him because he also had a long time interest in running. And he found all knowledge one should know about running for me (he is a Ph.D. in CS, so basically researching is his thing) and a schedule for beginners that should fit us just fine to get a head start. We all agreed on everything in two days and started running the day after.

Now we have reached our second week in training, and today I run for 4 minutes straight, then walk four minutes, and repeat four times. I feel that my body is gradually adapting to the run and getting a little better than yesterday. After all, the most critical measure is “better than yourself yesterday.”

Maybe it seems a little bit too trivial to mention, but all of these things would not have happened if I had not picked up my lazy body every morning that very first week to go running. And my friend would never offer me to start training with him, which helps me a lot for having another partner to get me up and endure the painful parts of the journey. It was truly wonderful to finally experience and understand the famous quote from “The Alchemist,”

When you want something, all the universe conspires in helping you to achieve it. – Paulo Coelho (The Alchemist)

I hope you also have something in your mind that you truly want to achieve. And you will finally achieve it, it all starts with you. Stay hungry and curious~ Hope you have a great day!

Love always,
Son
